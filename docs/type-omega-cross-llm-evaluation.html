Public Summary (as posted)

Reproducible Multi-Layer AI Architecture Across LLMs

The door is already open.
The question is — who still has the courage to walk through it?

This post reports an independent evaluation of the Type-Ω behavioral specification,
one concrete layer within the broader StarPolaris multi-layer architecture.

The goal is not to introduce a new model or capability,
but to test whether a prompt-level behavioral specification
can produce stable, reproducible reasoning behavior across LLMs.

Using a separate Claude instance, baseline LLM responses were compared
against Type-Ω across six test prompts and five behavioral invariants:
ontological minimality, contradiction containment, cross-format invariance,
epistemic transparency, and staged constraint propagation.

Key results show that staged constraint propagation (I5)
improved from 0% (baseline) to 100% (Type-Ω),
emerging as the clearest differentiating signature.
Overall achievement improved from 57.9% to 100%.

These findings suggest that Type-Ω functions as a reproducible,
prompt-level behavioral specification rather than a stylistic prompt.
